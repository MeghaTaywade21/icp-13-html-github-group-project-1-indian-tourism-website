About the kerla:

Kerala is a state located on the southwestern coast of India, along the Arabian Sea. It shares borders with Karnataka in the north and Tamil Nadu in the east.Kerala has a rich history
dating back over 3,000 years.It was an important spice trade hub, attracting traders from Greece, Rome, Arabia, China, and Europe.Colonized by the Portuguese, Dutch, and British,
you can still see their influence in cities like Kochi.It was formed on November 1, 1956, by merging Malayalam-speaking regions.Western Ghats on the east and Arabian Sea on the west.

Culture and People:

Kerala is known for its rich cultural heritage, classical arts like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, and religious harmony. The main language spoken is Malayalam. Important festivals include
Onam and Vishu.Kerala has the highest literacy rate in India (over 96%) and excellent education and healthcare systems.

  • Kathakali: Classical dance-drama with elaborate makeup and colorful costumes.
  • Mohiniyattam: Graceful solo female classical dance.
  • Theyyam: Ritualistic folk dance performed in temples and villages.
  • Ottamthullal and Koodiyattam: Other unique traditional performances.

Munnar - Popular hill station with tea gardens:

 

About the Munner:

Munnar is a picturesque hill station located in the Idukki district of Kerala, nestled in the Western Ghats at an altitude of approximately 1,600 meters (5,200 feet) above sea level. It lies at the
confluence of three mountain streams Mudrapuzha, Nallathanni, and Kundala from which it derives its name.Spend a day exploring Munnar and its surroundings with this private, guided tour
by taxi.You'll get the chance to see many of the hill town's most popular attractions, including various viewpoints and a tea plantation. You'll also have the chance to stop at Kundala Lake for
a bit of pedal boating before returning to your hotel

Major Attractions

  • Tea Plantations: Vast green estates with scenic walking trails.
  • Eravikulam National Park: Home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr and Neelakurinji flower (blooms every 12 years).
  • Top Station: Offers panoramic valley views and sunrise points.
  • Mattupetty Dam: Great for boating and picnics.
  • Echo Point: Famous for its natural echo phenomenon.

Activities to Enjoy

  • Tea estate tours and factory visits
  • Trekking and hiking through hills
  • Boating in lakes and dams
  • Photography in scenic viewpoints
  • Shopping for spices, tea, and chocolates

Kochi - Historic city with colonial architecture:

 

About kochi

Kochi (Cochin) is a vibrant city on the southwest coast of India in Kerala. It has been a major port since the 14th century and was colonized by the Portuguese, Dutch, and British, leaving
behind a legacy of European architecture, churches, and cultural fusion.Fort Kochi is a seaside area and part of the city of Kochi. It's known for its Dutch, Portuguese, and British colonial
architecture and bamboo fishing nets. Travellers can stroll through the cobbled streets of Fort Kochi, where colonial-era buildings, charming cafes, and vibrant street art create an atmosphere
of timeless beauty. The Chinese fishing nets along the coast add to the area's unique charm.

Major Attractions

  • Fort Kochi: Historic area with European-style streets and buildings
  • Chinese Fishing Nets: 14th-century nets introduced by Chinese traders
  • St. Francis Church: Oldest European church in India (built in 1503)
  • Mattancherry Palace (Dutch Palace):Showcases murals and royal heritage
  • Jew Town:Antique shops and the 400-year-old Paradesi Synagogue
  • Kerala Kathakali Centre: Cultural performances and martial arts shows

Kochi: Historic Architecture & Heritage

  • Dutch colonial buildings in Old Cochin — characterized by ornate stucco facades and verandahs, reflecting Dutch-era architecture
  • St. Francis Church, completed in 1516 — the oldest European church in India, once the burial site of Vasco da Gama
  • Fort Kochi waterfront warehouses and godowns — traditional European-style colonial-era warehouses along the coast
  • Portuguese-style heritage homes, such as Vicente House or Vasco House — whitewashed walls and wooden shutters signifying 16th-century European designs

Thekkady - Wildlife and Periyar Tiger Reserve:

About Thekkady

Thekkady, located in the Idukki district of Kerala, is one of India's top wildlife destinations. It is most famous for the Periyar Tiger Reserve, a protected area known for its rich biodiversity,
scenic Periyar Lake, and thrilling ecotourism activities.It's the perfect place for nature lovers, adventure seekers, photographers, and those wanting to experience Kerala's wild side.Located
in Thekkady, Idukki district, Kerala within the Western Ghats Covers approximately 777-925 sqkm of evergreen and moist deciduous forests Declared a wildlife sanctuary in 1950 and elevated
to a tiger reserve in 1978; core national park notified in 1982 Known for its Periyar Lake, formed by the Mullaperiyar Dam, and rich biodiversity including elephants, tigers, gaurs, sambar, and
over 260+ bird species

About Periyar Tiger Reserve

  • Established as a wildlife sanctuary in 1950 and declared a tiger reserve in 1978
  • Spread across 925 sq. km of forest area
  • Home to Bengal tigers, Indian elephants, gaurs, leopards, wild boars, and many birds
  • Famous for boat safaris on Periyar Lake, guided nature walks, and bamboo rafting

Do in Thekkady

  1. Boat Safari on Periyar Lake:
    • Best way to spot wildlife at the water's edge
    • Morning and evening cruises available
  2. Bamboo Rafting:
    • Silent and eco-friendly exploration of the forest
  3. Nature Walks:
    • Guided treks through evergreen and moist deciduous forests
  4. Night Patrol / Jungle Scout:
    • Forest guards lead you on night forest protection walks
  5. Elephant Rides & Interaction:
    • Ethical elephant feeding and bathing experiences
  6. Spice Plantation Visits:
    • Learn how cardamom, cinnamon, and pepper are grown
  7. Kalaripayattu & Kathakali Shows:
    • Local cultural performances every evening

Kovalam - Beautiful beaches:

About Kovalam

Kovalam is a beach and seaside resort in the city of Thiruvananthapuram in Kerala, India. Kovalam is located 12.6 km (7.8 mi) southeast of the city center. The beaches around Kovalam
are popular vacation destinations for both domestic and international travellersKovalam is a renowned beach town located about 16 kilometers south of Thiruvananthapuram, the capital
of Kerala. The word Kovalam means “a grove of coconut trees,” which accurately describes the landscape — the beaches are fringed with tall, swaying palms and golden sands.It first,
gained attention in the 1930s when the Maharani of Travancore invited European guests to experience its pristine shores. Later, in the 1970s, Kovalam became a favorite stopover for the
international hippie trail, gradually evolving into a vibrant beach tourist destination.

Main Beaches

  • Lighthouse Beach: Famous for the red-and-white striped Vizhinjam lighthouse offering amazing views.
  • Hawah Beach: Also known as Eve's Beach, popular for sunbathing and local culture.
  • Samudra Beach: Quiet and peaceful, mainly used by fishermen and locals.

Things To Do

  1. Swimming and Sunbathing
  2. Surfing and Parasailing
  3. Ayurvedic Massages and Spa
  4. Yoga Retreats
  5. Boat Rides and Local Market Shopping
