Some Top Historical Places In India That You Must Visit :

India is rich with historical places, ranging from ancient archaeological sites and magnificent temples to grand forts and colonial-era architecture, reflecting its diverse heritage and numerous dynasties. Notable examples include the Taj Mahal, a symbol of love and a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Agra, the Red Fort in Delhi, a UNESCO site and symbol of India's rich history built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, and the Qutub Minar in Delhi, known for its Indo-Muslim architecture and being one of the tallest free-standing stone towers in the world.

1.Ajianta &Ellora Caves, Maharashtra :


The Ajanta and Ellora Caves are two distinct but renowned UNESCO World Heritage Sites located in Maharashtra, India, celebrated for their exquisite rock-cut architecture and ancient art. The Ajanta Caves, primarily Buddhist, are famous for their vibrant mural paintings depicting Buddhist life and Jataka tales, while the Ellora Caves showcase a remarkable blend of Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain art and architecture, including the monolithic Kailasa temple.

  • Adderess :  Aurangabad, Maharashtra.
  • Year :         2nd century BC and 6th century AD.
  • Highlight : Ajanta predominantly featuring Buddhist monuments and paintings, while Ellora showcases a unique blend of Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain rock-cut temples. .
  • Visiting Time :  Ajanta Caves are open daily from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM, except for Mondays when they are closed.
                               Ellora Caves are open daily from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM, with the exception of Tuesdays when they are closed.
  • Ideal Time :  June to March, encompassing the monsoon and winter seasons, due to the pleasant weather conditions. While the monsoon season (June to September) offers lush landscapes and potential waterfalls, winter                       (November to March) provides cooler temperatures and comfortable exploration, avoiding the scorching summer heat of April and May.
  • Entry Fee :   For Indian citizens and visitors from SAARC and BIMSTEC countries = ₹40
                          Foreign tourists pay : ₹600
                          children under 15 years old : Free Entry
                          Online ticket prices are slightly lower : ₹35 for Indians/SAARC/BIMSTEC and ₹550 for foreign tourists..

2.Kangra Fort, Himachal Pradesh :


The Kangra Fort in Himachal Pradesh, built by the Katoch dynasty, is one of India's oldest and largest forts, tracing its origins to the ancient Trigarta Kingdom mentioned in the Mahabharata. It has a long and storied history, marked by numerous invasions and periods of rule by various dynasties, including the Mughals and the Sikhs, and was significantly damaged by a devastating earthquake in 1905.

  • Adderess :   Old Kangra, Kangra, Himachal Pradesh.
  • Year :           Over 3,000 years old.
  • Highlight :   Renowned for its strategic location and magnificent architecture, boasting a blend of ancient and medieval fortifications, including multiple gates, bastions, temples, and a historical museum.
  • Visiting Time :  Open from 9:00 AM to 5:30 PM daily, though some sources state a closing time of 6:00 PM or 5:00 PM.
  • Ideal Time :   From September to June, Summer months of May to June offer comfortable temperatures for trekking.
  • Entry Fee :    For Indian tourists = ₹150
                           For foreign tourists = ₹300

3.Golden Temple, Panjab:


The history of the Golden Temple, formally known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, in Amritsar, Punjab, spans from its foundation by Guru Ram Das in the 16th century to its eventual gold plating by Maharaja Ranjit Singh. It stands as the holiest shrine in Sikhism, built on principles of equality and accessibility for all.

  • Adderess :   Amritsar, Punjab.
  • Year :           In 1830.
  • Highlight :   Renowned for its stunning golden dome, serene Amrit Sarovar (holy pool), and intricate architecture blending Hindu and Islamic styles, serving as the spiritual heart of Sikhism. It's a major pilgrimage site and tourist                      attraction, celebrated for its inclusive ethos and the world's largest free community kitchen, the Guru ka Langar, which serves thousands daily.
  • Visiting Time :   open to visitors from 4:00 AM to 10:00 PM every day, though specific ceremonies like the Palki Sahib departure occur at earlier and later times.
  • Ideal Time :   Pleasant weather and vibrant festivities is between October and March, with the temple complex being particularly beautiful during festivals like Diwali (October/November) and Guru Nanak Jayanti (November).
  • Entry Fee :    There is no entry fee

4.Taj Mahal, Uttar Pradesh:



An ivory-white marble mausoleum commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal, and is considered a masterpiece of Mughal architecture and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is a symbol of love and a testament to the opulence and rich history of the Mughal Empire, featuring a complex with a mosque and guest house within formal gardens.

  • Adderess :   Agra, Uttar Pradesh.
  • Year :            Began in 1632, completed by 1638-39 & the entire complex finished in 1653.
  • Highlight :   Its highlights include its stunning architecture blending Indian, Persian, and Islamic styles, featuring intricate carvings, floral motifs, and precious stones, and its serene setting within a 42-acre complex with lush                        gardens and a reflecting pool.
  • Visiting Time :  The Taj Mahal is open daily except for Fridays when it is closed for prayers, with general visiting hours from 30 minutes before sunrise to 30 minutes before sunset.
                                Night viewing is also available on five nights a month: during the full moon, two days before, and two days after, from 8:30 PM to 12:30 AM.
  • Ideal Time :   During the cooler months from October to March, with early morning
  • Entry Fee :  For Domestic/Indian tourists = Rs.50
                         For citizens of SAARC and BIMSTEC countries = Rs.540
                         For Foreign tourists = Rs. 1100

5.Kamakhya Temple, Assam :



The Kamakhya Temple, located in Guwahati, Assam, is a highly revered Hindu shrine dedicated to the goddess Kamakhya, a manifestation of the mother goddess and a prominent Shakti Peetha. It's one of the oldest temples in India and is known for its connection to Tantric practices and the annual Ambubachi Mela, which celebrates the goddess's menstruation.

  • Adderess :   Nilachal Hill, near Guwahati city, Assam.
  • Highlight :   The highlights of Kamakhya Temple in Assam, one of India's most sacred and ancient Shakti Peethas, include its location atop Nilachal Hills in Guwahati, its dedication to Goddess Kamakhya (representing feminine power), the central significance of the Ambubachi Mela festival celebrating the goddess's menstruation, the unique yoni-shaped cleft in the inner sanctum, and its importance in Tantric practices.

6.KonarkSun Temple, Odisa :



The Konark Sun Temple in Odisha was constructed in the 13th century, around 1250 CE, by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty and is designed in the shape of a colossal chariot dedicated to Surya, the Sun God. This architectural marvel, a UNESCO World Heritage site since 1984, is renowned for its intricate carvings and symbolic representation of the sun god's celestial journey

  • Adderess :   Konark, Puri district, Odisha, India.
    • Year : 1250 CE
  • Highlight :   renowned for its unique chariot-shaped architecture designed to resemble the Sun God's divine chariot, with 24 intricately carved stone wheels serving as precise sundials and seven magnificent stone horses that appear to pull it forward
  • Visiting Time :   Open from 6:00 AM to 8:00 PM daily.
  • Ideal Time :   November to February.
  • Entry Fee : For Indian citizens and citizens of SAARC and BIMSTEC countries = ₹40
    . For other foreign visitors. = ₹600
    Children below 15 years of age and physically disabled visitors are granted free entry.
